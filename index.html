Goodbye, Duelyst

You were one of my favorite games

Cry With Me

The Game Was Great

Duelyst was a game by Counterplay Games, a studio that later dropped the game to develop Godfall. What made Duelyst special was the fact that it had all of the same cardplay goodness of Hearthstone along with the interesting positioning of chess... kind of. I think a more apt way to put it, if less widely-known and something that will likely get me labelled a nerd, is DnD style positioning. It's the kind of thing that really makes you think about how you've placed your units, in a way that Hearthstone only kind of captures, mixed with the dopamine hit of getting the card you needed to steamroll your opponent or trying to wriggle your way out of a tough situation when you got a bad roll. It was fun.

What Set It Apart

My first time plaing Duelyst was magical. It was beautiful, with incredibly detailed animated sprites and a free-to-play model that gave me a good chance of winning even if I didn't spend that much money on games. To 16 year old me, who didn't have any kind of credit card at the time (thankfully), it was like the chains being taken off. No longer did I have to labor for tens of hours just to build up the resources to build a deck so I could actually play the game. I was right there, in the middle of it! And boy was it fun. It's hard to really convey fun in writing. It was nail-biting to think I'd lost, just to eventually realize that I did have a way to finish off my opponent and eke out victory. It was interesting to see each game unfold, each person getting dealt a hand by the powers that be, each advantage being converted and where making the right play, often in this game theory-esque series of plays that were 10 layers deep, often made all the difference.

You might even say, "that just sounds like Hearthstone," to which I would say, "you're right." To me, though, it was better. The art was endearing, the sprites carefully animated to convey character, personality, and even culture. Each hit was satisfying, and the games were always short but fun. It was card-based games, distilled down to the best parts.

The truth is, though, I haven't been completely honest. There was a point where I had fallen off the train and stopped playing. Some personal things happened and I just never got back around to it.

And It's Gone Now

Recently, the game popped back into my mind. I checked the Steam page. The install button didn't work. I looked down at the announcements and to my surprise the game had been shut down. Gone. Forever.

I still think sometimes of what could've been.

Conclusions

Duelyst was a one-of-a-kind game for me. It was when everything that I liked in games lined up just right, in a way that's hard to get because not enough games like the games that I like.

It didn't really take off, but when it was here, I really enjoyed it. If someone did bring it back, it wouldn't be the same. But to me, at the time, Counterplay Games made something special, and I'm grateful.
